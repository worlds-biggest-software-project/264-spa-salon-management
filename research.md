## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **HIPAA** — required for medical spas handling patient health records, treatment plans, and before/after photography
- **PCI DSS** — payment card security standard for salon and spa point-of-sale and online booking deposits
- **GDPR / CCPA** — privacy compliance governing client personal data, consent management, and marketing opt-ins
- **GHS/SDS (Safety Data Sheets)** — chemical product safety standards relevant to retail and treatment product inventory tracking
- **ISO 31000** — risk management guidelines applicable to insurance and liability tracking for treatment-related incidents

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global spa and salon management software market operates within the broader wellness industry, which exceeded USD 6 trillion globally. Software-specific segments are estimated in the hundreds of millions, growing at approximately 10–12% CAGR driven by post-pandemic wellness demand and beauty service industry professionalisation.

**Funding:** Mindbody has raised over $380M across multiple rounds and was taken private by Vista Equity Partners. Boulevard raised $70M Series C in 2022. Fresha raised $100M at a $1B+ valuation in 2021. Zenoti raised $160M Series D in 2021.

**Pricing Landscape:** Ranges from free (Fresha, Square Appointments) with transaction fees to $24/month (GlossGenius) at the solo operator end, up to $400–470+/month for enterprise platforms (Zenoti, Mindbody, Boulevard). Most premium platforms require 12-month contracts.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Independent beauty professionals (solo estheticians, stylists) seeking simple scheduling; multi-chair salon owners managing staff commissions and retail; day spa operators coordinating therapist availability and treatment room utilisation; medical spa practitioners requiring HIPAA-compliant clinical documentation; hotel and resort spa directors integrating with property management systems.

**Notable Trends:** AI phone receptionists and chatbots handling inbound booking calls; no-show reduction through automated reminders and deposit requirements; retail product management and commission tracking tightly integrated with booking data; membership and package monetisation to smooth seasonal revenue; Zenoti's "AI First" approach with Zeenie assistant setting a new benchmark.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- AI receptionist that answers inbound calls, books appointments, manages reschedules, and collects deposits without human involvement
- Personalised client record intelligence — surfacing treatment history, product preferences, skin or hair notes, and birthday recognition automatically at appointment time
- Dynamic therapist scheduling that optimises room utilisation, minimises transition times, and accounts for treatment complexity and therapist certifications
- Predictive retail recommendations generated from treatment history and purchase patterns, delivered as personalised post-visit messaging
- Revenue forecasting and staff planning that predicts busy periods from historical booking data, local events, and seasonal trends to right-size the rota
